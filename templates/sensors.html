Keeping Bad Actors Out

Comes with threat filters built-in for threat mitigation including aggression detection/reporting and IMSI-Catcher detection/blocking.

Aggression

1M5 does not support aggression in any form by any entity. One major form of aggression spread on the internet is child abuse. The Aggression module inspects all images and videos for evidence of child abuse (and only child abuse). If it discovers such imagery, it removes the imagery from the message so that it does not reach its destination, re-encrypts the imagery, the sender's IP address and additional information using the 1st relay's key, then sends that message to the 1st relay whereby it will continue on the original message to its destination, minus the image while the new content containing the image, IP address of the sender, and additional information gets decrypted then sent directly to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children.

IMSI-Catcher

False mobile towers (base stations) acting between the target mobile phone(s) and the real towers of service providers. As such they are considered a Man-In-The-Middle (MITM) attack. This surveillance technology is also known as "StingRay", "Cellular Interception" and alike. The IMSI-Catcher Sensor discovers IMSI-Catchers and automatically blocks the phone from connecting to them. Future versions may support sharing of known IMSI-Catchers, honeypotting them to learn more about them, and using exploits to shut them down.

Aggression

1M5 does not support aggression in any form by any entity. One major form of aggression spread on the internet is child abuse. The Aggression module inspects all images and videos for evidence of child abuse (and only child abuse). If it discovers such imagery, it copies the imagery, re-encrypts it with the sender's IP address and additional information using the 1st relay's key, then sends the message to the 1st relay whereby the original message will continue on to its destination while the copied imagery, IP address of the sender, and additional information gets sent directly to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children. The reason we allow the imagery to continue on is to not raise suspicion among the exchangers of the illicit imagery in the hopes of catching them. Imagery is evidence of wrong-doing. The act is what needs to be stopped and the imagery is the evidence to help find, rescue, and prosecute the attackers.

IMSI-Catcher

False mobile towers (base stations) acting between the target mobile phone(s) and the real towers of service providers. As such they are considered a Man-In-The-Middle (MITM) attack. This surveillance technology is also known as "StingRay", "Cellular Interception" and alike. The IMSI-Catcher Sensor discovers IMSI-Catchers and automatically blocks the phone from connecting to them. Future versions may support sharing of known IMSI-Catchers, honeypotting them to learn more about them, and using exploits to shut them down.
